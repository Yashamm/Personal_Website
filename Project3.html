Direct Ink Writing (DIW) of smart ceramics and high throughput computational modeling for parameter selection


Mentor - Dr. Zoubeida Ounaies (PSU)

Objective: Direct Ink Writing (DIW) is predominant in industries such as biomedical ana electronics. Parameter selection becomes critical in any additive manufacturing process. Since the complexity of solidification, melting, etc., is absent in DIW, the computational model becomes useful. However, simulating such a deposition process is computationally expensive. In this study, parameter selection space is created using a multi-fidelity surrogate model that minimizes the computational expense and minimal hit-and-trial experiments.
